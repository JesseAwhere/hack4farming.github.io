Judges

Stephen Muchiri

Mr. Muchiri has experience in the agricultural sector in a number of fields, including work in sustainable agricultural and rural development, micro and macro-economic policies and regional integration, bilateral and multilateral trade negotiations (WTOs, EPAs); agricultural development research, and development of rural community organizations. He is curently the Chief Executive Officer of the Eastern Africa Farmers Federation, where he works to steer the organization to promote the interests of farmers on issues touching on policies, markets, productivity, capacity, information and regional integration. The EAFF also works to voice the legitimate concerns of farmers, promote socio-economic development through strengthening of individual country platforms, developing positions on regional issues, advocating for member organisations, and promoting sustainable agricultural and rural development.

His areas of specialization are agriculture trade, agribusiness clusters development, capacity building of farmers, advocacy, agriculture information & advisory services, partnerships, gender mainstreaming, and environmental fields.

Leonard Otieno Oruko

Dr. Oruko is an agricultural economist with 22 years' progressive experience in applied economics research, strategic planning, and performance monitoring. He has previously worked for the Ethiopian Agricultural Transformation Agency (ATA), the Forum for Agricultural Research in Africa (FARA), the Association for Strengthening Agricultural Research in Eastern and Central Africa (ASARECA), CAB International, and the International Livestock Research Institute. He now runs an independent consulting firm. Dr. Oruko holds a PhD in Agricultural Economics from the University of Reading (UK), an M.Sc. in Agricultural Economics, and a B.Sc. in Agriculture from the University of Nairobi (Kenya).
